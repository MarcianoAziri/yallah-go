Talisker Whisky Atlantic Challenge

The premier event in ocean rowing - a challenge that will take us more than 3000 miles (4800 km) west from San Sebastian in La Gomera, Canary Islands to Nelson's Dockyard English Harbour, Antigua & Barbuda.

The annual race begins in early December, with up to 30 teams participating from around the world. The fastest row across the Atlantic was a 4 man team, The Four Oarsmen, who finished in 29 days, 14 hours and 34 minutes. The average time for a 4 man crew is between 30 and 40 days.

Some facts we will be faced with:

1.5 million oar strokes

2h rowing, 2h rest, 24/7

Atantic storms

Severe dehydration

Shark infested waters

Blisters and salt sores

40 degree heat

Exhaustion and hallucinations

Sleep deprivation

Waves up to 20ft high

Another challenge: Summary costs

To be able to raise money for the NOMV foundation, we need to get to the start-line in La Gomera. First of all we need a safe boat, need to do 4 courses (first aid at sea, sea survival, short range radio licence and navigation/ seamanship) Next to these courses we need to row in team for 120h, as we are from 4 different countries this will take some well planning. This is why we take part of the race in 2024, giving us time to practice and time to find our sponsors.
